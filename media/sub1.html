ABOUT

" Seoul Story "

  • Seoul began as the first capital of Baekje and was known as Hanyang during the Joseon Dynasty. In the early 20th century, it was renamed Gyeongseong during Japanese occupation and became Seoul after the Korean War. Developing around the Han River, Seoul has grown into the political, economic, and cultural center of South Korea. Key landmarks include Gyeongbokgung Palace, Namdaemun, and Bukchon Hanok Village. Today, Seoul is known as a global metropolis that harmonizes tradition and modernity.

    As a crucial transportation hub, Seoul has played an important role from ancient times to the present. The city features a blend of historical heritage and modern architecture. Seoul hosts various international events, enhancing its status as a global city. Economically, areas like Gangnam have become centers of advanced industries. These elements together make Seoul a beloved tourist destination.

" Seoul History "

Seoul's history begins with its establishment as Wiryeseong, the first capital of Baekje, and it became known as Hanyang when it was designated as the capital of the Joseon Dynasty in 1394. During the Japanese occupation, it was renamed Gyeongseong, and after the Korean War, it firmly established its name as Seoul. In modern times, it has developed into a global metropolis and the political, economic, and cultural center of South Korea.

Seoul has been a crucial transportation hub since ancient times, developing around the Han River. The city is rich in historical heritage, including palaces like Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung, as well as cultural sites such as Namdaemun and Bukchon Hanok Village. Despite rapid urban development, Seoul maintains a harmony between tradition and modernity, sustaining its status as a global city.
