ABOUT

  • Seoul, Capital of South korea has long been essential site from three kingdom period until now Thanks to its geographic advantage. So it has been the capital of many kingdoms on the korean peninsula. The Han River in Seoul was important for increasing productivity in the agricultural society, useful for defending themselves against external enemies, and for trading with other regions in Korea and abroad.

    And now Through modernization and urbanization, This great city with a population of more than 10 million has embraced both traditional beauty by preserving its royal palaces and modernity through fancy skyscrapers. So Seoul is considered as the city where the past and present coexist. From fashion to food, Seoul is a trendy city that hasit all.

  •
    Gyeongbokgung Palace
    Gyeongbokgung Palace, established in 1395, was the Joseon Dynasty's foremost royal residence, epitomizing its authority in Seoul.
  •
    Changdeokgung Palace
    Changdeokgung Palace, built in 1405, is Seoul's second UNESCO-listed palace, celebrated for its beauty and harmony with nature.
  •
    Changgyeonggung Palace
    Changgyeonggung Palace, built in 1483, housed kings' wives, linked to Changdeokgung Palace. Restored after Japanese invasion, returned to original form in 1987.
  •
    Deoksugung Palace
    Deoksugung Palace blends medieval and modern styles, featuring a western garden and the traditional Changing of the Royal Guard. Visitors love its picturesque surroundings.
  •
    Gyeonghuigung Palace
    Gyeonghuigung Palace, built in 1623, reopened in 2002 after restoration. It now hosts the Seoul Museum of History and Seoul Museum of Art's Gyeonghuigung Annex.

SPECIAL

Seoul, the vibrant capital of South Korea, is filled with captivating attractions. From the iconic N Seoul Tower offering panoramic city views to the soaring heights of Lotte World Tower, and the modern design of Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP), there are diverse landmarks to explore. With its rich culture, dynamic energy, and modern amenities, Seoul promises an unforgettable experience for every traveler.

  • Namsan Tower
    Namsan Seoul Tower, built in 1969 as Korea's first integrated transmission tower, broadcasted TV and radio across Seoul. Opened to the public in 1980, it has become a beloved city landmark.
  • Lotte Tower
    As of April 2024, standing at 555 meters with 123 floors above ground, this building holds the titles of South Korea's tallest, the world's sixth tallest, and the tallest in the OECD.
  • DDP
    The purpose of DDP is to use design-thinking for innovation and development. Additionally, DDP introduces global design trends and innovations as a hub of 21st-century creative knowledge.

Seoul boasts a variety of parks, offering tranquil spaces for relaxation. Yeouido Hangang Park, stretching along the Han River, provides opportunities for riverside walks and cycling. Additionally, Haneul Park and Olympic Park offer beautiful natural landscapes intertwined with cultural experiences, perfect for unwinding and rejuvenating.

  • Yeouido Hangang Park
